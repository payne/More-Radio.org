## Area Repeaters

Repeaters extend the range of VHF and UHF communications by receiving signals and retransmitting them at higher power from elevated locations. Here's a guide to repeaters in our area.

### How Repeaters Work

A repeater simultaneously receives on one frequency (input) and transmits on another (output). The difference between these frequencies is called the "offset." Most repeaters also require a CTCSS tone (PL tone) to activate.

### Repeater Etiquette

1. **Listen before transmitting** - Make sure the repeater is not in use
2. **Identify properly** - Use your callsign per FCC regulations
3. **Keep transmissions brief** - Leave gaps for others to break in
4. **Yield to emergencies** - Emergency traffic always has priority
5. **Use simplex when possible** - Don't tie up the repeater unnecessarily
6. **Avoid kerchunking** - Don't key up without identifying
7. **Be courteous** - Treat others as you'd want to be treated

### Programming Your Radio

Most modern radios can be programmed via computer software. Common tools include:

- **CHIRP** - Free, open-source programming software
- **RT Systems** - Commercial software for many brands
- **Manufacturer software** - Brand-specific programming tools

Need help programming your radio? Ask at a club meeting - we're happy to help!

### Linked Systems

Some repeaters are linked together, allowing communication over a wider area:

- **Regional links** - Connect repeaters across multiple counties
- **Internet linking** - IRLP, EchoLink, AllStar connections
- **Digital networks** - DMR, D-STAR, Fusion networks

### Repeater Directories

For a comprehensive list of repeaters:

- **RepeaterBook** - repeaterbook.com
- **ARRL Repeater Directory** - Available from ARRL
- **RFinder** - Mobile app for finding repeaters

### Supporting Local Repeaters

Repeaters require ongoing maintenance and expenses. Consider:

- Joining the sponsoring club
- Making a donation to repeater maintenance
- Volunteering for site maintenance
- Reporting problems promptly
