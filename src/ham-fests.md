## Area Ham-Fests

Ham-fests are gatherings where amateur radio operators buy, sell, and trade equipment, attend forums, take license exams, and enjoy fellowship with other hams. Here's a list of ham-fests in our area.

### What to Expect at a Ham-Fest

- **Flea market** - Tables full of new and used equipment
- **Commercial vendors** - Major manufacturers and dealers
- **Forums & presentations** - Educational sessions on various topics
- **VE testing** - License exam sessions
- **Prize drawings** - Often including radios and accessories
- **Food & refreshments** - Often available on-site

### Annual Events

#### Dayton Hamvention

The largest amateur radio gathering in the world, held annually in May near Dayton, Ohio. Thousands of hams attend this three-day event featuring hundreds of vendors, forums, and the famous flea market.

#### Local Swap Meets

Our club hosts periodic swap meets where members can buy, sell, and trade equipment. These smaller events are great for finding deals and meeting local operators.

### Tips for Ham-Fest Attendees

1. **Bring cash** - Many vendors prefer cash, especially at flea markets
2. **Arrive early** - The best deals go fast
3. **Bring a wagon or cart** - Helpful for hauling purchases
4. **Test before you buy** - If possible, verify equipment works
5. **Bring your license** - Required for VE testing and some purchases
6. **Dress for the weather** - Flea markets are often outdoors
7. **Bring a list** - Know what you're looking for
8. **Network** - Ham-fests are great for meeting other operators

### Selling at Ham-Fests

Interested in selling your surplus equipment? Most ham-fests offer table rentals for sellers. Contact the event organizers for details on:

- Table costs and availability
- Setup times and rules
- Electrical power access
- Load-in/load-out procedures
